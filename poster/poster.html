🔥 Motivation

Biological sequence design — finding DNA or protein sequences with high fitness — is a central challenge in computational biology. Surrogate models accelerate optimization by learning to approximate expensive oracle measurements.

But optimizers actively push candidates into regions far from training data. Do surrogates remain reliable there?

⚠️ Challenge

Surrogates trained on low-fitness sequences must generalize to the high-fitness sequences optimizers find. This distributional shift, quantified by minimum Hamming distance from the training set, is unavoidable in practice.

Overconfident predictions in OOD regions mislead optimizers and waste oracle calls.

💡 Research Questions
  • RQ1: How well do surrogate models (MLP, RF) generalize as Hamming distance from training data increases?
  • RQ2: How effectively do different optimization strategies find high-fitness sequences when guided by a surrogate?
🧬 Datasets
  • TFBind8 — DNA 8-mers; exhaustive oracle (65,536 sequences); binding to transcription factors. Continuous, near-linear landscape.
  • GB1 — Protein G domain; 4-site combinatorial mutations; sparse, epistatically complex fitness landscape.
  • Both: surrogates trained on bottom 50% by fitness score.

⚙️ Surrogate Models & Optimizers
Surrogate Models
  • MLP — Multi-layer perceptron on one-hot encoded sequences. Flexible, can extrapolate more aggressively (wider prediction distribution: σ = 0.071 on TFBind8 vs. RF σ = 0.033).
  • RF — Random Forest; ensemble averaging constrains predictions toward training mean, limiting extrapolation range (max prediction 0.424 vs. MLP 0.645 on TFBind8).
Optimizers
  • SMW (Naive) — Random-mutation walk scored by surrogate; simple but exploits overconfident predictions.
  • Goal-Directed RL — Generative LSTM trained via RL with surrogate as reward; broader exploration.
  • GFlowNet — Samples proportional to fitness; promotes candidate diversity alongside quality.

Key findings: (RQ1) Surrogate performance is dataset-dependent: MLP strongly outperforms RF on GB1 (ρ 0.65 vs. 0.33) but is slightly weaker on TFBind8 (ρ 0.27 vs. 0.37). Both models degrade monotonically as minimum Hamming distance from training data increases. Both exhibit systematic positive bias — consistently overestimating fitness in OOD regions — a structural consequence of training exclusively on low-fitness sequences, not an artifact of model choice. (RQ2) Goal-directed RL substantially outperforms the naive SMW baseline on TFBind8 (0.96 vs. 0.81 max oracle score), demonstrating the value of learned generation strategies.
