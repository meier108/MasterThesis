🔥 Motivation

Biological sequence design — finding DNA or protein sequences with high fitness — is central to drug discovery, enzyme engineering, and synthetic biology.

Surrogate models approximate expensive experimental measurements (oracle calls) to guide optimization cheaply.

But optimizers actively push candidates into out-of-distribution (OOD) regions far from training data. Do surrogates remain reliable there?

🧬 Datasets

TFBind8 — DNA 8-mers, exhaustive oracle (65,536 seqs). Binding to transcription factors. Near-linear, continuous fitness landscape.

GB1 — Protein G domain B1, 4-site combinatorial mutations (56k generated seqs). Sparse, epistatically complex landscape.

Train regime: Surrogates see only the bottom 50% by fitness — realistic proxy for early-stage optimization where high-fitness examples are unknown.
⚙️ Surrogate Models

MLP — Multi-layer perceptron on one-hot sequences. Flexible; extrapolates more aggressively (prediction σ = 0.071 on TFBind8).

RF — Random Forest; ensemble averaging constrains predictions toward the training mean (σ = 0.033; max pred 0.424 vs MLP 0.645 on TFBind8).

🤖 Optimizers (RQ2)
  • SMW — Single-mutant walk; random mutations scored by surrogate. Simple baseline.
  • RL-LSTM — Generative LSTM trained via RL with surrogate reward; broader exploration.
  • GFlowNet — Samples proportional to fitness score; promotes candidate diversity (Jain et al., 2022).

Research Question 1 How well do surrogate models generalize as Hamming distance from training data increases?

Surrogates are trained on low-fitness sequences, but optimizers push candidates further away. We measure generalization by computing predictions on holdout sequences stratified by minimum Hamming distance from the training set, then comparing to oracle scores.

💡 Key Insights — RQ1

RF clearly outperforms MLP at high Hamming distance (ρ 0.32 vs 0.04 at HD=4). RF's ensemble averaging acts as a natural OOD regularizer, preventing aggressive extrapolation — whereas MLP collapses almost entirely at HD=4.

Both models show a systematic negative bias: they consistently underestimate the fitness of OOD sequences. Since training data is restricted to the bottom 50%, surrogates have never seen truly high-fitness examples and regress toward the low-fitness mean — producing conservative but systematically wrong predictions in OOD regions.

Research Question 2 How effectively do different optimization strategies find high-fitness sequences when guided by a surrogate?

💡 Key Insights — RQ2

On TFBind8: GFlowNet > RL-LSTM > SMW. Learned generation strategies substantially outperform the naive mutation walk by exploring broader sequence space rather than greedily following surrogate gradients.

On GB1: the RL-LSTM achieves an anomalously high oracle score of 9.32 (vs SMW at 1.24), exposing critical surrogate exploitation. On this epistatically complex landscape the MLP surrogate is unreliable at high Hamming distance (as RQ1 confirms), and the RL policy learns to generate sequences that maximally fool the surrogate — not genuinely high-fitness sequences. This directly links RQ1 and RQ2: surrogate breakdown at high OOD distance translates to optimizer failure in complex landscapes.
